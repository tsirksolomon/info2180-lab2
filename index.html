Test Tube is a Stubborn Character

Date: 2023/10/17

(I pulled this from some of a post I made elsewhere for the sake of having a paragraph)
So earlier I said that I'm of the opinion that Test Tube has spent most of the Inanimate Insanity series being the type of character that can become stubborn and aggressive when she thinks she's in the right and that it's one of her best and worst traits, and then I also sort of joked about her being a hater. I wanted to compile some scenes I thought illustrated that point.

I thought it was appropriate and a little silly to hae the first clip being from Season 2, Episode 10, where upon seeing ghost Bow, she is so distraught at the fact that she was incorrect about something that she shatters. The idea of someone dying because they can't conceive of being wrong sometimes is hilarious, but I also included it because I think it's where the groundwork for the aforementioned traits are first laid.(I will not be adding the video in this cut for GitHub version of this entry).

Incessantly Chronicling Others' Behaviour

Date: 2023/10/17

(I also pulled this from a post I made elsewhere for the sake of having a paragraph.)
Imagine a hypothetical scenario wherein you participate in the third installment of a televised, survival-based competition, and part of your winning strategy is documenting the behaviour of the other contestants and using that information to your advantage. Keeping research on all sorts of other topics also happens to be something you enjoy doing, so you have some information that may be useful for the competition, but a lot more that is less likely to be. Additionally, you need to document things because you have problems with your memory, but the rest of the cast is unaware of this. All of this data is with you at all times.

Are other contestants justified if they find your behaviour scary or off-putting? If you trust a friend to hold on to some of this data and they break your trust by distributing it to other contestants without asking you in a bid to get you eliminated, not because you're a threat, but because they think you are a bad person, are they justified?
